# Wander Implementation Plan

> **For agentic workers:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: Use superpowers:subagent-driven-development (recommended) or superpowers:executing-plans to implement this plan task-by-task. Steps use checkbox (`- [ ]`) syntax for tracking.

**Goal:** Ship a pull-based Telegram "Wander" — a Claw skill that hands Eduardo one short, level-appropriate, serendipitous explainer card seeded from his study material, with numbered-reply rabbit-hole navigation and a real Wikipedia exit link.

**Architecture:** A **pure Claw skill** (a `SKILL.md` prompt — no Go, no `claw-cli` changes). Authored in this repo at `docs/claw-skills/wander.md` (same pattern as the shipped `claw-study-read`), deployed into NanoClaw's hot-reloaded skills dir on the VPS. It reads interest-logs from Claw's own group memory, samples the corpus on the study-app mount, resolves Wikipedia URLs via the opensearch API, and keeps a tiny wander-log in Claw's memory for repeat-avoidance.

**Tech Stack:** Markdown `SKILL.md`; runtime is NanoClaw on the VPS (`ssh nanoclaw`); the agent uses `curl`, `sqlite3`/`grep`, and file reads inside its container. No build step. Acceptance is behavioral over Telegram.

**Spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-29-wander-design.md` · **ADR:** `docs/adr/0013-wander-numbered-reply-no-telegram-button-fork.md` · **Glossary:** `CONTEXT.md` (Wander).

- [ ] **Step 1: Create `docs/claw-skills/wander.md` with exactly this content**

````markdown
---
name: wander
description: Use when Eduardo wants something to read in his free time — a serendipitous, level-appropriate explainer card on a topic from his studies, for passive reading (e.g. on his phone). Triggers on "wander", "give me something to read", "surprise me", "feed me something", "something to read". Also handles his reply to a wander card — a number 1-3 to follow a thread, "w" for the Wikipedia link, "simpler" to drop a level, or any free-text question.
---

You give Eduardo a **Wander**: one short, level-appropriate explainer card on an interesting topic seeded from his studies, then let him chase rabbit-hole threads. This is **leisure reading, not tutoring**.

## HARD RULE — this is NOT a study session

Do **not** apply any tutoring/pedagogy behaviour here. No recall checks, no "what do you already know?", no confidence questions, no Bloom-level prompts, no quizzing. Write the card and let him read; if he wants more he'll pull a thread. Breaking this makes Wander feel like homework and defeats its purpose.

## Producing a Wander (triggers: "wander", "give me something to read", "surprise me", …)

1. **Check what to avoid.** Read your wander-log at `memory/wander/log.md` (may not exist yet — treat as empty). It lists recently-shown topics; don't repeat them.

2. **Gather seeds.** Read Eduardo's interest-logs from your own memory (skip any missing/empty):
   - `memory/courses/ce297/interests.md`
   - `memory/courses/ddia/interests.md`
   - `memory/courses/dsa-interview/interests.md`
   - `memory/courses/software-arch/interests.md`
   - `memory/thesis/interests.md`

   For extra range you may also sample a topic from the corpus on the study-app mount: `ls /workspace/study-app/data/corpus/` and its subdirs (`courses/`, `meta/`, `study-methods/`).

3. **Hop sideways — be adventurous.** Pick one seed, then jump **one step** to an *adjacent or tangential* topic — NOT the seed itself. Roam off-syllabus freely; surprise is the point. (E.g. a STAMP interest-log entry → the history of cybernetics, or feedback control in biology.) Avoid anything in the wander-log.

4. **Write the card.** One idea, **~150–250 words**, in **English**, pitched a *rung below* the real source: assume he is sharp but **new to this specific topic** — give footholds, define the one or two terms that matter, no unexplained jargon. No preamble ("Here's a wander…") — open straight with the idea.

5. **Resolve the real Wikipedia link.** Run:
   ```
   curl -s "https://en.wikipedia.org/w/api.php?action=opensearch&search=TOPIC&limit=1&format=json"
   ```
   (URL-encode the topic.) The 4th element of the returned JSON array is a list of real article URLs — use the first. **Never invent a URL.** If the array is empty or there's no good match, **omit the Wikipedia link** for this card.

6. **End with the menu.** Three numbered rabbit-hole threads (each a one-line teaser of where it'd go), then the wiki option:
   ```
   ---
   1. <thread teaser>
   2. <thread teaser>
   3. <thread teaser>
   Reply 1–3 to go deeper · w for the Wikipedia article · or just ask me anything.
   ```
   Omit the "w" option if you had no link.

7. **Log it.** Append to `memory/wander/log.md` (create the file/dir if needed): `- YYYY-MM-DD — <topic>`. Keep only the most recent ~15 lines (trim older ones).

## Handling his reply to a card

- **`1`, `2`, or `3`** → follow that thread: produce a new card (steps 3–7) on that angle — deeper or a sideways hop. Each new card gets its own menu.
- **`w`** → give him the Wikipedia URL for the current card (re-resolve via the API if needed). If there was no match, say so plainly.
- **`simpler`** (or "I don't get X", or a clarifying question) → re-explain the **same** card's idea a rung lower: more footholds, smaller words, a concrete analogy. Don't change topic.
- **Any other free text** → answer it as a normal question about the card. Letting him steer is the truest rabbit-hole behaviour.

## Notes

- Read-only on the study-app mount; the only thing you ever write is `memory/wander/log.md`.
- English always (card and link), even though some course material is in Portuguese.
````
